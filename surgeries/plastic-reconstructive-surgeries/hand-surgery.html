What is Hand Surgery?

Hand surgery is a specialized medical procedure aimed at addressing issues in the hand, wrist, and forearm. It can involve repairing injuries, correcting deformities, treating diseases, or enhancing functionality of the hand. Hand surgery is performed by orthopedic surgeons, plastic surgeons, or hand specialists, depending on the complexity of the issue. The surgery may involve bones, tendons, muscles, nerves, skin, or joints and can range from minimally invasive procedures to more complex reconstructive surgeries.

How to diagnose Hand Surgery? When to consult a doctor?

Diagnosis of hand conditions that may require surgery involves a thorough physical examination and may include imaging tests and other assessments by a healthcare professional, usually an orthopedic surgeon or hand specialist.

You should consult a doctor if:

  • Persistent Hand Pain
  • Limited Function or Range of Motion
  • Injuries
  • Numbness or Tingling
  • Visible Deformities or Lumps

Frequently Asked Questions

Physical therapy is often recommended after hand surgery to help restore strength, flexibility, and function. The length and intensity of therapy depend on the type of surgery performed.

Recovery time varies depending on the type of surgery, but it can range from a few weeks to several months. Physical therapy may also be required to regain strength and mobility.

Many hand surgeries, especially minimally invasive procedures, are done on an outpatient basis, allowing patients to go home the same day.
