What is Bone Marrow Transplant?

A bone marrow transplant (BMT) is a medical procedure that involves replacing damaged or diseased bone marrow with healthy bone marrow stem cells. Bone marrow is the soft tissue found in the center of bones that produces blood cells, including red blood cells, white blood cells, and platelets. A BMT can be a life-saving treatment for various conditions, especially those affecting blood cell production.

How to diagnose Bone Marrow Transplant? When to consult a doctor?

Diagnosing the need for a bone marrow transplant (BMT) typically involves several steps to evaluate the underlying condition affecting the bone marrow or blood cells. The diagnostic process: Medical History, Physical Examination, Blood Tests, Bone Marrow Biopsy & Imaging Tests.

You should consult a doctor if:

  • Unexplained Fatigue: Persistent fatigue that doesn't improve with rest may indicate anemia or other blood-related issues.
  • Frequent Infections: Recurring infections or an inability to fight off infections can be a sign of low white blood cell counts or immune dysfunction.
  • Easy Bruising or Bleeding: Unexplained bruises, frequent nosebleeds, or prolonged bleeding from cuts may indicate low platelet counts or clotting issues.
  • Bone or Joint Pain: Persistent pain in the bones or joints can be associated with various blood disorders and may warrant further investigation.
