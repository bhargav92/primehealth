What is Mastectomy / Lumpectomy?

A mastectomy is a surgical procedure that removes one or both breasts, either partially or entirely, typically to treat or prevent breast cancer. In contrast, a lumpectomy is a breast-conserving surgery where only the tumor or abnormal tissue is removed, along with a small margin of healthy tissue. The objective of a lumpectomy is to eliminate the cancerous cells while preserving as much of the breast as possible. Radiation therapy usually follows to target any remaining cancer cells.

How to diagnose Mastectomy / Lumpectomy? When to consult a doctor?

The decision to undergo a mastectomy or lumpectomy is typically based on the diagnosis of breast cancer or other serious breast conditions. Diagnosis involves several steps: Physical Examination, Mammogram, Ultrasound, MRI (Magnetic Resonance Imaging), Biopsy & Genetic Testing.
