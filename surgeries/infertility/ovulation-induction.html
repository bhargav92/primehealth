What is Ovulation Induction?

Ovulation induction is a medical treatment used to stimulate the ovaries to produce and release eggs (ovulation) in women who have difficulty conceiving due to ovulatory disorders. It is often a part of infertility treatment for women with irregular or absent ovulation, which can make it challenging to achieve pregnancy.

How to diagnose Ovulation Induction? When to consult a doctor?

The diagnosis and assessment process typically involves several steps: Medical History, Physical Examination, Ovulation Tracking, Blood Tests, Ultrasound, Hysterosalpingography (HSG) & Laparoscopy.

You should consult a doctor if:

  • Irregular Menstrual Cycles: If your periods are irregular or absent, it may indicate ovulatory issues that require assessment and treatment.
  • Inability to Conceive: If you have been trying to conceive for over a year (or six months if you’re over 35), it may be time to consult a doctor.
  • Signs of Hormonal Imbalance: Symptoms like excessive hair growth (hirsutism), acne, weight gain, or breast tenderness can indicate hormonal issues affecting ovulation.
  • Previous Reproductive Health Issues: If you have a history of conditions such as polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS), endometriosis, or previous pelvic surgery, you should seek evaluation.

The process can vary; typically, it may take one to several cycles to achieve ovulation, and treatment may continue for several months, depending on the individual’s response.

Risks include multiple pregnancies (twins or more), ovarian hyperstimulation syndrome (OHSS), and side effects from medications, such as mood swings or hot flashes.

Patients can expect regular monitoring, medication administration, and timing of intercourse or insemination around the predicted ovulation.
