What is Varicose Vein Surgery?

Varicose vein surgery is a medical procedure aimed at treating varicose veins, which are enlarged, twisted veins that typically appear on the legs and feet. These veins occur when the valves within the veins weaken or fail, causing blood to pool and the veins to bulge. While many varicose veins are harmless, they can sometimes cause discomfort, pain, or complications.

How to diagnose Varicose Vein Surgery? When to consult a doctor?

Diagnosing varicose veins usually involves a combination of a physical examination and, if necessary, diagnostic tests.

You should consult a doctor if:

  • If your varicose veins cause aching, heaviness, or throbbing pain in your legs, particularly after standing or sitting for extended periods.
  • Swelling in the legs or ankles.
  • Changes in skin color (darkening or discoloration), particularly around the ankles.
  • If you experience frequent leg cramps or restless leg syndrome, especially at night, which may be linked to varicose veins.
  • If a varicose vein ruptures and starts bleeding, it may require urgent treatment, as this indicates the veins have become fragile and prone to injury.

Frequently Asked Questions

Recovery varies by procedure, but most patients can resume normal activities within a few days. Walking is encouraged to promote blood flow, and strenuous activities should be avoided for a couple of weeks.

Varicose vein surgery is highly effective, with success rates of 90-95%, particularly for minimally invasive techniques like EVLT and RFA.

Risks include infection, bruising, swelling, blood clots, and nerve damage. However, complications are rare, especially with minimally invasive procedures.
